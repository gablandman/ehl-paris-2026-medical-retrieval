## Report

**Goal:** Fine-tune BrainIAC for cross-modal same-subject retrieval on the 350 dataset1 pairs, testing (A) frozen-backbone adapter head and (B) LoRA, to try to beat 0.555.

**Result: honest negative — neither method beat 0.555, and neither beat the frozen BrainIAC baseline (0.307).**

### What I found and fixed
The interrupted run had completed the head mode but **trained nothing** — loss was stuck at exactly `ln(32)=3.467` and holdout MRR ~0.13. Root cause (diagnosed by probing the backbone): the **frozen BrainIAC feature is near-collapsed** — every input maps to almost the same 768-d vector (off-diagonal cosine ~0.9999; a huge shared offset with a tiny per-sample residual). Fed raw into a head, InfoNCE can't separate anything.

The fix I added: **per-dimension feature standardization** (subtract dataset mean / divide by std, computed once from the backbone, stored as buffers) before the head. After this, both modes train cleanly (loss 5.1→0.2; in-pool holdout MRR reaches ~0.45–0.51 on 40 pairs).

### Leaderboard comparison (public MRR)
| variant | argmax | sinkhorn | hungarian |
|---|---|---|---|
| frozen BrainIAC | 0.307 | 0.233 | 0.288 |
| **adapter head (A)** | 0.261 | 0.268 | 0.288 |
| **LoRA r=8 (B)** | 0.299 | 0.296 | 0.292 |
| SliceCLIP (best) | 0.481 | **0.555** | 0.529 |

### Interpretation
- Both modes **memorize** dataset1 (biased train MRR: head 0.54–0.68, LoRA 0.86–0.92) but **do not transfer**. LoRA memorizes harder (more capacity) yet generalizes no better.
- The public LB is dominated by dataset2/3 pools, which have **no training labels** and a likely domain shift. Adapting on dataset1 alone overfits that one dataset.
- LoRA edges out the head slightly and is the more robust of the two, but both are statistically on par with the frozen encoder.

**Recommendation:** Fine-tuning BrainIAC on dataset1 alone is a dead end here. The frozen-encoder + strong reranker recipe (SliceCLIP + Sinkhorn, 0.555) remains the target. Closing the gap needs signal on dataset2/3 (labels, or self-supervised cross-modal alignment per dataset), not more adaptation on dataset1.

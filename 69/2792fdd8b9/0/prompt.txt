## Report

**Goal:** Fine-tune BrainIAC for cross-modal same-subject retrieval on the 350 dataset1 pairs, testing (A) frozen-backbone adapter head and (B) LoRA, to try to beat 0.555.

**Result: honest negative — neither method beat 0.555, and neither beat the frozen BrainIAC baseline (0.307).**

### What I found and fixed
The interrupted run had completed the head mode but **trained nothing** — loss was stuck at exactly `ln(32)=3.467` and holdout MRR ~0.13. Root cause (diagnosed by probing the backbone): the **frozen BrainIAC feature is near-collapsed** — every input maps to almost the same 768-d vector (off-diagonal cosine ~0.9999; a huge shared offset with a tiny per-sample residual). Fed raw into a head, InfoNCE can't separate anything.

The fix I added: **per-dimension feature standardization** (subtract dataset mean / divide by std, computed once from the backbone, stored as buffers) before the head. After this, both modes train cleanly (loss 5.1→0.2; in-pool holdout MRR reaches ~0.45–0.51 on 40 pairs).

### Leaderboard comparison (public MRR)
| variant | argmax | sinkhorn | hungarian |
|---|---|---|---|
| frozen BrainIAC | 0.307 | 0.233 | 0.288 |
| **adapter head (A)** | 0.261 | 0.268 | 0.288 |
| **LoRA r=8 (B)** | 0.299 | 0.296 | 0.292 |
| SliceCLIP (best) | 0.481 | **0.555** | 0.529 |

### Interpretation
- Both modes **memorize** dataset1 (biased train MRR: head 0.54–0.68, LoRA 0.86–0.92) but **do not transfer**. LoRA memorizes harder (more capacity) yet generalizes no better.
- The public LB is dominated by dataset2/3 pools, which have **no training labels** and a likely domain shift. Adapting on dataset1 alone overfits that one dataset.
- LoRA edges out the head slightly and is the more robust of the two, but both are statistically on par with the frozen encoder.

**Recommendation:** Fine-tuning BrainIAC on dataset1 alone is a dead end here. The frozen-encoder + strong reranker recipe (SliceCLIP + Sinkhorn, 0.555) remains the target. Closing the gap needs signal on dataset2/3 (labels, or self-supervised cross-modal alignment per dataset), not more adaptation on dataset1.

## Cross-attention reranker (FINDINGS axis D2) — Negative result

**Verdict: it does NOT beat 0.555.** The cross-attention reranker scores **0.464 (Sinkhorn) / 0.395 (argmax)** versus the bi-encoder baseline's **0.555 / 0.481** — about 0.09 below, on real leaderboard submissions (all 6 pools, 377 rows verified).

### What I built (`xattn_rerank.py`)
On top of the frozen SliceCLIP bi-encoder: each volume → 64-channel conv feature grid → adaptive-pool to 16 tokens; query and candidate tokens cross-attend bidirectionally; the resulting match logit is added as a **learned residual on top of the bi-encoder cosine** (so it starts exactly at the 0.555 baseline). Trained on the 350 dataset1 pairs with in-batch listwise softmax, frozen backbone, dropout + weight decay. Includes the synthetic-dataset2 transfer harness (independent rigid+elastic deformation of held-out pairs) that the task asked for.

### Leaderboard comparison

| Method | argmax | Sinkhorn |
|---|---|---|
| SliceCLIP bi-encoder (baseline) | 0.481 | **0.555** |
| Cross-attention reranker, no aug | 0.395 | 0.464 |
| Cross-attention reranker, ds2-style aug | 0.394 | 0.452 |

### Why it fails (two diagnosed, mechanistic causes)
1. **The conv tokens barely vary across subjects.** Measured directly: the 64-ch conv grid has per-feature std ~0.006 across volumes (nearly constant), while the 128-d projected embedding has std ~0.088 and separates clean ds1 perfectly (MRR≈1.0). The signal lives in the projection MLP, not the tokens — so a plain cross-attention head fed those tokens **collapsed to a constant score** (top-1 conflict 1.0). Anchoring on cosine fixed the collapse but left little to add.
2. **The learned refinement overfits ds1 and scrambles ds3.** With the cosine anchor it leaves ds1/ds2 rankings unchanged (top-1 agreement 1.0) but **flips ~90% of dataset3 top-1 picks** (agreement 0.10) — ds3 is the out-of-distribution pre→intraoperative pool where cosine is least confident, so the ds1-trained refinement overwhelms the weak signal there. **Synthetic-ds2 transfer check: Δ MRR = +0.000** (refinement converged to exactly the cosine ranking on deformed holdout) — the offline proxy correctly predicted "no gain" before I spent submissions.

### Process note (important)
A `/code-review` pass caught a **load-bearing gradient bug** in my first version: the trainable cross-attention params were computed inside the backbone's `torch.no_grad()`, so the head never trained (only `base_scale`/`refine_gate` updated). I fixed it (verified gradients now flow; also fixed a dead-gate at `refine_gate=0`), re-ran, and the conclusion held — the gradient-fixed v3 scored 0.464, identical to the buggy v2. So the negative result is robust, not an artifact.
